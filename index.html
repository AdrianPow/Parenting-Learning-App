Weigh it like an economist

Oster never tells you what to choose. She tells you how to structure the choice: data in, preferences in, opportunity cost counted. This tool walks you through it.

The method in one story

Post-baby, Oster couldn't cook dinner from scratch anymore. Take-out? Meal kit? Two dinners? She priced each option, added the value of her own time ("opportunity cost"), then let preferences break the tie. Same data, different families, different right answers. Breastfeeding and sleep training work exactly the same way.
