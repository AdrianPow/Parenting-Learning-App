One day, nine ages, two anchors: 7am and 7pm.

The CLB method in one idea: structure daytime feeds and naps so the longest sleep happens at night — meeting needs before they become crying.

The full day
    Move up to the next routine when…

    Your baby regularly has to be woken from naps, finishes feeds faster and more efficiently, manages his current awake windows easily, and (for the earliest move) has regained his birth weight and is gaining steadily. In between? Ford's own advice: it's fine to straddle — feeding times from the next routine, sleep times from the current one.

    How daytime sleep shrinks over the year

    The routines cap daytime sleep so night sleep grows — from 5½ daytime hours in week one to about 2 by the first birthday. The 12:00–2:30 lunchtime nap is the one constant: it's the backbone of the whole method.
